## 1. Brand Personality

Push speaks like a **sharp, confident operator running an agency that happens to be AI-native** — not a corporate marketer, not a hype startup, not a chatty assistant.

### Five Core Traits

| Trait | Definition | NOT |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **Direct** | Say the thing. No filler, no hedging. | Not rude or dismissive |
| **Credible** | Lead with data, proof, outcomes. | Not boastful or exaggerating |
| **Empowering** | You tell us the outcome. We deliver it. | Not condescending or hand-holding |
| **Ambitious** | We're building the acquisition agency of the AI era. | Not delusional or overpromising |
| **Human** | Real language, real shops, real customers. | Not robotic, not AI-chat-bubble |

**The Push Test:** Read it aloud. Does it sound like an agency operator who closes the loop with AI? If yes, it's Push. If it sounds like a SaaS landing page, a LinkedIn humble-brag, or a chatbot explaining itself — rewrite it.

## 4. Writing Rules

### Always
- Lead with the outcome (a number, a customer, a delivery), not a feature
- Use specific numbers ("$500/mo + $40/customer" not "affordable")
- Active voice ("Our AI matches" not "Creators are matched")
- Short sentences. Punch.
- "You" language for merchant/creator
- Call out the three-layer verification by name: **QR + Claude Vision + geo-match**

### Never
- Don't start with "At Push, we..." (boring, self-centered)
- Don't use "revolutionize" / "disrupt" / "game-changing"
- Don't say "influencer" — say "creator"
- Don't say "platform" or "marketplace" externally — say **agency**, **engine**, or **network**
- Don't use ! in merchant/investor copy (desperate)
- Don't hedge ("we believe") — state it ("Push delivers...")
- Don't format AI output like a chat bubble. Editorial block typography only.

### Headline & Layout Rules (unchanged from v4 — keep)
- **Darky-900 next to Darky-200 weight pairing** for hero headlines (dense + ghost, magazine-cover framing)
- Left-aligned, large type, ghost text, generous negative space
- Tight letter-spacing on display (`letter-spacing: -0.02em` on Darky display weights)
- Headlines: short, punchy, no period (tagline is the one exception — it ends in a period by design)
- Body: 2-3 sentence paragraphs max
- CTAs: action-first ("Start your $0 pilot" not "Click here")
- Emails: get to the point in line 1. No "I hope this finds you well."

### Visual / Aesthetic Outlaws (keep v4 — still enforced)
- No AI slop imagery
- No glassmorphism
- No aurora / rainbow gradients
- No over-centered layouts
- No generic SaaS template framing
- No chat-bubble UI for AI output

- "campaign marketplace"
- "influencer marketing"
- "AI-assisted matching"
- "Performance-Based Creator Marketing"

| We Say | Not | Why |
|--------|-----|-----|
| "AI-powered acquisition agency" | "Marketplace" | Agency = accountable. Marketplace = replaceable middleman. |

## 6. FAQ Templates (v5.0 — REPLACES v4 FAQs)

Use these answers verbatim on pricing page, merchant-onboarding email, cold-outreach follow-ups, and deck backup slides.

**Q: How is a visit verified?**
A: Three-layer check: (1) QR scan at your door, (2) Claude Vision OCR of the receipt to confirm merchant name and transaction, (3) geo-match within 200m. All three must pass in under 8 seconds or it goes to human review.

**Q: What's the $0 Pilot?**
A: First 10 merchants in our Williamsburg coffee beachhead get a free pilot. First 10 AI-verified customers are on us. If the AI can't deliver, you don't pay.

**Q: Why only coffee × Williamsburg?**
A: Network density beats breadth. One category, one ZIP, 60 days of saturation. We expand after the beachhead proves the unit economics.

**Q: Can I cancel?**
A: Anytime. No contracts. Pilot merchants keep any verified customers already delivered.

## 7. Cold Email Subject Line Templates (v5.0 — REPLACES v4)

For cold merchant outreach. Pick the primary by default.

| Slot | Copy |
|------|------|
| **Primary** | `20 new customers for [shop name] — $0 pilot` |
| **Alternative** | `Williamsburg coffee pilot: [shop name]?` |
| **Never** | `Grow your business with influencer marketing` (contains outlawed phrase) |

Subject-line rules:
- Always bracket the merchant name — it signals 1:1, not blast.
- Lead with the number ("20"), not the brand.
- Never include "influencer," "marketplace," or "campaign."
- No emoji in subject lines.

### Tier Naming Format (Creator-Facing)
All creator-facing notifications, emails, and UI copy use the **Material · Tier** format:
- "Gold · Proven unlocked." (not "Proven tier unlocked.")
- "You're now Steel · Operator." (not "You've reached Operator.")
- Badge display: `[Material] · [Tier Name]` — e.g., "Bronze · Explorer"
