# Push Creator System — v5.1

> **Terminology note (v5.1):** Push is **Vertical AI for Local Commerce**. The creator network is structured as **Two-Segment Creator Economics** — the earning model differs fundamentally between T1-T3 (pay-per-verified-customer) and T4-T6 (retainer + performance + rev-share + equity). The phrase "operator network" from v5.0 is retired; the replacement conceptual frame is the **Creator Productivity Lock** described under the **SCOR framework** below. "Operator" remains the material name of tier 3 (Steel) within the ladder.

## Philosophy
"Anyone can start. Performance determines how Push's agents route you, how much you earn per verified customer, and — at the top — how much equity and rev-share you unlock."

Creators are valued by **verified conversions delivered** (not follower count), completion rate, reliability, merchant satisfaction, content quality, and FTC disclosure compliance. Campaigns are **routed by the matching agent reading ConversionOracle**, not browsed by creators.

## Two-Segment Creator Economics (v5.1 — AUTHORITATIVE)

The creator ladder splits into two economically distinct segments. The earning model and retention mechanics are fundamentally different between them; this is the single most important v5.1 change.

### Segment A — T1-T3: Pay-per-verified-customer

Entry-level, time-flex, no commitment. Creators earn a per-customer rate that varies by vertical. Designed for accessibility: any eligible creator can start at T1 and progress based on performance.

| Tier | Material | Follower band | Per-customer rate (by vertical) | Typical monthly | Key unlock |
|------|----------|---------------|----------------------------------|------------------|-------------|
| **Seed** | Clay | 5K – 15K | coffee $12 / coffee+ $20 / dessert $18 / fitness $48 / beauty $68 | 10-30 customers, **$150-400 side income** | Zero barrier entry |
| **Explorer** | Bronze | 15K – 30K | coffee $15 / coffee+ $25 / dessert $22 / fitness $55 / beauty $75 | **$300-600** | CreatorGPT brief assist |
| **Operator** | Steel | 30K – 50K | coffee $20 / coffee+ $35 / dessert $30 / fitness $60 / beauty $80 | **$600-1,500** | Commission 3% active; priority routing inside tier; cross-vertical access |

Design target: T1-T3 is the accessibility layer. Creators in this segment do not receive a monthly retainer — earnings are 100% tied to verified customer delivery. Milestone bonuses (unchanged from v4.1 → `+$15 @ 30 txns/mo` for Operator) remain in force as acceleration incentives.

### Segment B — T4-T6: Retainer + Performance Bonus + Rev-share + Equity

Professional tier. Creators in this segment receive a monthly base retainer from a merchant-pooled fund, earn per-customer performance bonuses on top, and — from Closer up — participate in the long-run upside of the merchants they refer via rev-share and equity.

| Tier | Material | Follower band | Base retainer | Perf bonus per customer | Rev-share | Equity | Typical monthly |
|------|----------|---------------|----------------|--------------------------|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Proven** | Gold | 30K – 80K | **$800/mo** | **$25** | none | none | $1,500-2,000 |
| **Closer** | Ruby | 80K – 250K | **$1,800/mo** | **$40** | **15%** of referred merchant Push revenue, 12-mo window, **$500/mo cap per merchant** | **0.02% equity pool** per top-100 Closer, 4-yr vest + 1-yr cliff, perf-gated after year 2 | **$3,000/mo blended** |
| **Partner** | Obsidian | 250K+ | **$3,500/mo** | **$60** | **20%** (same 12-mo window + $500/mo cap) | **0.05-0.2% equity pool** with lock-up | **$5,000+/mo**; exclusive enterprise campaign access |

**Rev-share mechanic (Closer/Partner):** For every merchant a Closer/Partner refers onto a Push subscription, the creator earns a % of Push's recognized monthly revenue from that merchant for 12 months from sign-up. Capped at $500/mo per merchant, per creator, to prevent concentration. Attribution tracked via signed referral agreement + merchant self-attestation at sign-up.

**Equity pool gating (Closer/Partner):** Eligibility for equity requires sustained FTC DisclosureBot compliance ≥ 98%, no dispute penalties in trailing 90 days, and active tier status. Equity is vested quarterly; a demotion below tier 5 within the vest window pauses vesting (does not forfeit already-vested equity).

**Why two segments?** T1-T3 optimizes for supply density (many creators, low friction); T4-T6 optimizes for ambassador density (few creators, deep alignment). The economics of $5-8 coffee transactions make a pure per-customer model illegible for professional creators; the retainer+equity layer aligns them with the merchant's long-run success, not just the next transaction.

## Creator Productivity Lock — SCOR framework (v5.1 — replaces "operator network" framing)

The reason creators stay on Push — and the reason competitors cannot replicate the economics — is not tier status or pay rate. It is the **Creator Productivity Lock**: the density, matching, ops, and reputation compounding that lets a Push creator out-earn the same creator on Aspire or TikTok by 2-3× per hour. Described via the **SCOR** framework:

### S — Supply density
- **Target:** 50+ merchants per neighborhood rotation
- **Measured proof:** 5.2 active campaigns/mo on Push vs Aspire 1.8, TikTok 0.9
- **Why it locks:** A creator switching to a competitor drops from 5.2 to 1.8 campaigns/mo instantly. Supply density is the first-order lock.

### C — Conversion-aware matching (ConversionOracle)
- **Engine:** ConversionOracle predicts walk-in conversion for a creator × merchant × geo × time combination **before** the campaign is routed.
- **Outcome:** Creator earning/hour on Push is 2-3× the earning/hour on rival platforms because mismatched campaigns are filtered out pre-route.
- **Why it locks:** A creator's conversion history on Push is context-rich (merchant×geo×time×vertical); a competitor cannot replicate this without Push's telemetry. The rating is portable (tier label); the context is not.

### O — Operations leverage
- **CreatorGPT** — AI brief assist unlocked at Explorer+: reads the invite brief and auto-drafts the content angle, CTA, and disclosure.
- **DisclosureBot** — AI pre-screens every creator post for FTC #ad disclosure before publication (see compliance section below).
- **48h payout** — industry norm is 30-60 days. Push pays in 48h.
- **Net effect:** Creators save ~**70% ops time** per campaign vs rival platforms.

### R — Reputation portability (asymmetric)
- **Portable externally:** The tier label (Seed / Explorer / Operator / Proven / Closer / Partner) IS portable — creators can reference it on their IG/TikTok bio, in pitch decks, with other brands.
- **NOT portable:** The ConversionOracle context — the per-vertical affinity, the geo×time×merchant conversion profile, the `verified_conversions_90d` ledger — lives only on Push. A competitor cannot replicate a creator's proven conversion rate without Push's scan data.
- **Why it locks:** Creators get the best of both worlds (external credibility + internal stickiness), and they can feel the asymmetry.

**Competitive comparison (v5.1 reference):**

| Dimension | Push | Aspire | TikTok Shop | Traditional brokerage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Supply density (campaigns/mo) | **5.2** | 1.8 | 0.9 | 0 (manual brokerage) |
| Conversion-aware matching | Yes (ConversionOracle) | No | No | Human judgment |
| Ops leverage (CreatorGPT + DisclosureBot) | Yes | Partial | No | No |
| Payout speed | **48h** | 30d | 60d | 45-90d |
| Creator earning/hour (indexed) | **2-3x** | 1.0 | 0.8 | 1.2 |

## How matching works (v5.1 — ConversionOracle-driven)

1. **Merchant inputs a goal** via the Pilot/Subscription portal (e.g., "100 first-time customers at 11211 coffee shop, 30 days").
2. **Matching agent (Claude Sonnet 4.6)** queries ConversionOracle with the goal + merchant category + geo + tier entitlements.
3. **Top 5 creators** per goal are selected. They receive a DM invitation with a CreatorGPT-drafted brief. No open board, no application queue.
4. **Creator accepts or declines** in-DM within a **2-hour response window**. Declined slot triggers the agent to surface the next candidate. End-to-end from merchant input to creator invite: **under 60 seconds**.

### What the agent reads when ranking

Tier is the coarsest filter (merchant plan entitles access to specific tier bands — see "Merchant Tier Linkage" below). Within the entitled band the agent reads:

| Signal | Source | Role in v5.1 routing |
|--------|--------|----------------------|
| **Tier** | Push Score → tier band | Determines routing priority + per-customer rate + retainer eligibility |
| **Push Score** | Composite formula (see `scoring-model.md`) | Overall quality proxy (unchanged 0-100 composite) |
| **`category_affinity`** (0.0-1.0 per vertical) | Automated classification of last 90d content + campaign history | Drives per-vertical fit; recomputed nightly |
| **`verified_conversions_90d`** | Rolling 90-day count of AI-verified customers | North-star output metric; tiebreaker + profile badge |
| **ConversionOracle predicted conversion** | Model output for this creator × merchant × geo × time | Core ranking input — pre-filters mismatches |
| **Distance to merchant** | Creator home geo → merchant geo (miles) | Hyperlocal verticals weight < 2 mi heavily |
| **Last-30d availability** | Current concurrent campaigns vs. tier cap | Prevents inviting saturated creators |
| **DisclosureBot compliance rate (trailing 90d)** | Auto-screen pass rate | Gates equity pool eligibility; below 98% = no equity, tier risk |
| **Anti-fraud flags** | Device/IP/self-referral history | Hard exclusion |

### Williamsburg coffee priority (current beachhead)
Until we graduate beachhead, the agent applies a priority lane:
- `coffee_affinity > 0.7` AND `distance_miles < 1.2` from zip 11211

Creators meeting both conditions get **first-pass routing** for any Williamsburg coffee merchant goal, regardless of tier. A Seed-tier creator with 0.91 coffee affinity living 4 blocks from the merchant often outperforms a higher-tier creator living 3 miles away — ConversionOracle knows this.

### What did NOT change in v5.1
The tier ladder, material colors, Push Score formula, commission percentages for T1-T3 (3/5/7% at Operator/Proven/Closer — note: Proven/Closer commission from v4.1 is preserved but superseded at T4+ by retainer+performance model for practical earnings), milestone bonuses, demotion grace window, anti-gaming rules, and base pay × Campaign Difficulty Multiplier are **unchanged**. v5.1 changes the earning model at T4-T6 and the framing of the lock mechanism; the scoring core is intact.

### Commission + Milestone Bonus — T1-T3 acceleration layer
**Problem solved:** Pure percentage commission on $5-8 F&B transactions yields negligible absolute value (3% of $6 = $0.18). Commission alone cannot serve as the "behavioral inflection point."

**Solution: Percentage + Referral Milestone Bonus (hybrid model, T1-T3 active layer)**
- **Percentage commission** applies per transaction (3% at Operator — the first tier where it activates)
- **Referral Milestone Bonus** unlocks when creator's referral transactions hit a monthly threshold

| Tier | Commission % | Milestone Threshold | Milestone Bonus |
|------|-------------|--------------------|-----------------| 
| Operator | 3% | 30 transactions/month | +$15 |
| Proven | 5% (legacy) | 40 transactions/month | +$30 |
| Closer | 7% (legacy) | 60 transactions/month | +$50 |
| Partner | 10% (legacy) | 80 transactions/month | +$80 |

**Concrete example — Operator:**
- 40 referral transactions × $7 avg = $280 total referral value
- Commission: $280 × 3% = $8.40
- Milestone bonus (hit 30+): $15
- **Total referral income: $23.40/month** (vs. $8.40 without milestone)
- Plus per-customer rate at $20 coffee base × say 15 customers = $300
- Operator total: ~$323/month on light activity

At Proven+, the $800/mo retainer + $25/customer perf bonus becomes the dominant earning mechanism; milestone bonuses remain as acceleration but are no longer the inflection point (the retainer is).

### 30-Day Attribution Window (unchanged)
- All transactions through a creator's referral link within 30 days count toward commission + milestone
- Window resets if consumer uses a different creator's referral link (last-click attribution)
- Creator dashboard shows real-time referral count + distance to milestone

### Seed Tier — Zero-barrier entry (unchanged)
- **Zero barrier:** No follower count requirement below 5K. Just register + fill profile.
- **15-20 slots per campaign** (high volume, low cost) — preserves supply density for the Creator Productivity Lock
- **Upgrade path:** Complete 2 campaigns + provisional score ≥ 40 → auto-promote to Explorer
- **Upgrade nudge:** After completing 1st campaign, creator receives "$5 cash bonus" on 2nd campaign completion as acceleration incentive

### Progression Philosophy — v5.1
Each tier unlocks exactly what the previous tier's creators start craving. The v5.1 shift: at the segment boundary (Operator → Proven), the earning model itself changes, which is the biggest progression moment in the entire ladder.

- Seed → Explorer: Official identity + cash payment + referral data transparency + CreatorGPT brief assist
- Explorer → Operator: Commission + milestone bonus + cross-vertical access (behavioral inflection point within T1-T3)
- **Operator → Proven: Retainer unlock — earnings become predictable ($800/mo base floor) — the segment-crossing inflection**
- Proven → Closer: Retainer jump to $1,800/mo + rev-share + equity pool eligibility (the wealth-compound inflection)
- Closer → Partner: Retainer to $3,500/mo + 20% rev-share + larger equity pool + exclusive enterprise campaign access

### Merchant Tier Linkage — v5.1 mapping

Agent routing respects tier entitlements defined by the merchant's current plan:

**Merchant plan → creator tier band accessible to agent routing:**
- **Pilot ($0, 30-day trial):** Seed + Explorer + Operator (T1-T3 only); agent routes up to 3 invites per goal
- **Subscription ($500/mo min + $40/customer):** All tiers eligible; agent routes up to 5 invites per goal, with Proven+ priority
- **Scale tier (higher retainers):** All tiers + founder can request specific Closer/Partner creator by name; up to 8 invites per goal

**Creator → Merchant preference (unchanged):**
- **Proven+:** Can set preference to prioritize Subscription-tier merchants
- **Closer+:** Can filter to only accept Scale-tier merchant goals
- **Partner:** Can lock in exclusive merchant partnerships (1-3 merchants); agent routes matching goals first

## FTC DisclosureBot compliance (v5.1 — NEW gate)

Every creator post is **AI pre-screened for FTC #ad disclosure** before publication goes live on-platform record. DisclosureBot checks:
- Presence of `#ad`, `#sponsored`, or equivalent FTC-compliant disclosure
- Placement (must be in first 3 lines of caption, not buried)
- Visibility on story/reel overlay (not hidden behind swipe)
- Clarity for non-English caption markets (Spanish/Chinese equivalents)

**Enforcement ladder:**
| Offense | Consequence |
|---|---|
| 1st bypass (creator posts without disclosure, flagged by DisclosureBot) | Payout forfeit for that campaign + tier demotion (drop one tier) + mandatory disclosure retraining |
| 2nd bypass (within trailing 12 months) | Permanent ban from platform + forfeit of any unvested equity (Closer/Partner) |

**Compliance rate gating:**
- **DisclosureBot compliance rate** is tracked per-creator as trailing 90-day pass rate
- **≥ 98% compliance** required for equity pool eligibility (Closer/Partner)
- **< 95%** triggers tier re-evaluation (not automatic demotion, but flagged for review)
- **Visible on creator dashboard:** "Your DisclosureBot compliance rate: 99.2% — equity eligible"

Rationale: FTC enforcement risk scales with creator volume. DisclosureBot is the line between Push being a compliant channel and a liability; the compliance rate becomes a tier-retention and equity-eligibility signal, not just a back-office check.
