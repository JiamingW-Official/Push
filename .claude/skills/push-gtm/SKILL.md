# Push v5.1 GTM & Growth — Overview

## Launch Philosophy (v5.1)

**Single beachhead. Single category cluster. Single ZIP cluster. Single quarter of saturation.**

Push v5.1 is **Vertical AI for Local Commerce**. The product is the Neighborhood Playbook (codified AI-driven acquisition template) priced on outcomes ($0 Pilot → $500/mo min + $40/verified customer on Performance). The GTM loop only works if we saturate one neighborhood with one category cluster before anything else. Premature expansion kills density, density kills margin leverage (Software Leverage Ratio), and SLR is how we prove Vertical AI for Local Commerce to YC Summer 2027.

> **Never launch "a city." Never launch "Brooklyn coffee." We are launching Williamsburg Coffee+.**

## Software Leverage Ratio (SLR) — Primary North Star

**SLR = campaigns run per week ÷ human-ops hours per week.**

SLR is how we prove Vertical AI for Local Commerce is real software leverage and not a dressed-up services shop. ConversionOracle (verification / matching AI) and DisclosureBot (compliance automation) are the two production AI systems that drive SLR up as volume grows.

### v5.1 SLR Ladder (30-week)

| Phase | Week | SLR Target | Driver |
|-------|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 — AI MVP + first merchant | W1–2 | **3–5** | Baseline, mostly manual ops, ConversionOracle in staging |
| 2 — Cohort fill, first AI-matched campaign | W3–4 | **6** | ConversionOracle live in production, matching loop automated |
| 3 — Saturation, first merchant auto-flips to Operator | W5–8 | **8** by Week 8 | DisclosureBot live; 20 active campaigns with same ops headcount |
| 4 — Retention + Greenpoint prep | W9–12 | **10** by Week 12 | Auto ROI statements, standing-order campaigns |
| 5 — Adjacent expansion (Greenpoint + Bushwick) | W13–20 | **15** by Week 20 | Neighborhood Playbook replication without per-ZIP engineering |
| 6 — YC S27 prep | W20+ / M5 / M12 | **18 by M5, 25 by M12** | Full ConversionOracle + DisclosureBot at steady state |

**SLR tripwire:** if SLR stalls below target for 2 consecutive weeks, the next week's work is AI/automation, not merchant acquisition. Density without leverage is services, not software.

## Pilot Economics Framework (v5.1)

**Purpose:** cap per-neighborhood Pilot spend, enforce shared risk with creators, and make Pilot-to-Paid conversion a measurable product metric instead of an aspiration.

### Per-Neighborhood Pilot Cost Cap

| Item | Cap |
|------|-----|
| **Per-neighborhood Pilot cost cap** | **$4,200** |
| **Creator payout during Pilot** | 70% of standard rate (shared risk) |
| **Pre-Pilot LOI** | $1 nominal + 60-day commitment + case-study authorization |
| **Pilot-to-Paid conversion target** | **60%+** |
| **Auto-terminate clause** | Pilot auto-terminates at day 30 if merchant has < 5 verified customers |

**Why these numbers:**
- **$4,200 cap** — covers 10 Pilot merchants × ~$420 fully-loaded cost (creator payouts at 70%, ConversionOracle API cost, ops) with a small buffer for anchor-shop case-study production. If we blow past $4,200 in one neighborhood, the Neighborhood Playbook is broken and we stop, not spend more.
- **$1 LOI** — legally binding commitment without capital friction. Filters out tire-kickers; aligns merchant on 60-day runway.
- **60% Pilot-to-Paid** — math-driven. Below 60% the CAC on Paid merchants is too high to hit positive unit economics by M12.
- **Day-30 auto-terminate** — protects runway. If we can't deliver 5 verified customers in 30 days, the Neighborhood Playbook is mis-matched for that merchant.
- **70% creator payout during Pilot** — aligns creator incentive to care about Pilot-to-Paid conversion (creators earn full rate only once merchant flips to Paid).

## Launch Experiments (v5.1 — run every 2 weeks)

Every experiment has a control group of 5 and a treatment group of 5, runs for 14 days, and reports into the public Williamsburg dashboard.

| # | Experiment | Control | Treatment | Metric |
|---|------------|---------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **Merchant pricing positioning** | Current Operator pitch | Alternative pitch (outcome-first vs. price-first) | Pilot-to-Paid conversion % |
| 2 | **Creator compensation model** | Fixed $20 base per campaign | Variable 3% commission on verified customers | Creator completion rate + CAC |
| 3 | **ConversionOracle geo threshold** | 200m geo radius pass window | 180m vs. 250m comparison | Verified-customer false-positive rate |
| 4 | **DisclosureBot compliance test** (new in v5.1) | Manual disclosure review | DisclosureBot auto-generated FTC-compliant copy | 90-day rolling compliance pass rate (target ≥ 98%) |

## Retention Design (v5.1)

### Merchant Side
- Outcome-based pricing (no verified customers → no charge beyond $500/mo floor)
- Monthly ROI statement (auto-generated, emailed)
- Real-time dashboard at `/neighborhoods/williamsburg-coffee`
- One-click "run another campaign" (habit loop)
- Case-study features (social proof + press)

### Creator Side
- 24h fast payout (Venmo / Zelle)
- Push Score tier progression (Seed → Partner)
- Limited-seat campaigns (FOMO)
- Standby queue (near-miss)
- Non-portable score (loss aversion)
