# Push Strategy — Overview (v5.1)

> **v5.1 hardening (2026-04-18):** v5.1 is not a new strategy — it is the diligence-hardening pass applied after a YC partner mock review of v5.0. Ten structural gaps in v5.0's narrative were converted into quantified answers. The v5.0 reset to an outcome-priced, AI-driven customer acquisition business stands; v5.1 fixes how that story survives a 60-minute VC diligence meeting. Anywhere v5.0 and v5.1 conflict, v5.1 wins.

> **Language hard rule:** The word "agency" is banned from all external-facing Push materials (pitch deck, landing page, YC application, investor memos). v5.1 replaces it with **Vertical AI for Local Commerce** (primary) and **Customer Acquisition Engine** (product). The v4.x vocabulary — marketplace, SaaS tiers, $19.99, Performance-Based pricing, flat $40/customer — remains retired.

---

## Strategic Definition (v5.1)

Push is **Vertical AI for Local Commerce** — a customer acquisition engine that delivers verified walk-in customers to SMBs on outcome-aligned pricing. The merchant tells Push how many customers they need; the platform's agents match an AI-operated creator network, run the campaign, verify every visit with multi-modal AI (Claude Vision + QR OCR + geo), and bill per verified customer. Creators are not independent supply — they are operators on the Push network, locked in by supply density, conversion-aware matching, productivity tooling, and 48-hour payout.

**Push IS:** vertical AI for local commerce, a customer acquisition engine, a verification layer, an autonomous campaign engine, a ConversionOracle data asset, a creator productivity platform.

**Push is NOT:** an influencer marketplace, an agency, a creator SaaS tool, a local discovery app, a content community, a loyalty app, a sample-giveaway engine, a directory. (See "Strategic Red Flags" below for ban-list language.)

**v5.1 one-line pitch:** "Tell us how many customers you need. Our agents + creator network deliver — verified, or free."

## Five Strategic Pillars (v5.1)

### 1. Outcome Engine
Merchants tell Push **how many customers they need** (e.g. "20 new customers, this week, at my Williamsburg coffee shop"). Push commits to delivery or the campaign is free. Billing unit is the **verified customer** with vertical-matched pricing — Coffee+ $25, pure coffee $15, boutique fitness $60, beauty $85 — never impressions, followers, reach, or posts. Retention Add-on adds $8 (2nd visit ≤30d) and $6 (3rd visit ≤60d) on pure software margin.

### 2. Creator OS (Two-Segment)
Creators are Push's operator network, not marketplace supply. T1–T3 earn per verified customer (side income, $150–$750/month typical). T4–T6 earn retainer + performance + 12-month revenue-share on referred merchants + equity pool — a Tier 5 Closer targets **$3,000/month blended**. Switching cost is real and quantified via the **SCOR framework** (Supply density, Conversion-aware matching, Operations leverage, Reputation portability). Creator tier label is a signal, not a moat — the moat is 2–3× higher $/hour vs Aspire/TikTok Creator Marketplace.

### 3. AI Verification + DisclosureBot (Day-1)
Every claimed visit runs through multi-modal verification (Claude Vision + OCR + geo-fence + velocity/graph fraud) with 80% auto-approve / 20% human override as the deliberate quality floor. Every post runs through **DisclosureBot** — platform-level AI pre-screen for 16 CFR Part 255 compliance; creators cannot publish a Push campaign post without required disclosure tokens in correct position. Compliance is architectural, not behavioral.

### 4. Agentic Campaign Engine
Merchant submits a goal → in ~60 seconds the agent returns a creator slate + campaign brief + ROI prediction. Campaign design, matching, brief generation, pricing, and timing are agent-driven from campaign #1. Humans handle strategy and exceptions.

### 5. ConversionOracle™ Data Moat
Every verified visit generates a labeled training record: `(creator_profile, content_style, merchant_category, neighborhood, offer, time, weather) → verified_walk_in, customer_LTV`. This walk-in ground truth is exclusive to Push — Meta/Google/TikTok never see it, Apple Wallet only has large chains. The training flywheel (500 → 1K → 10K → 50K events) plus Creator Productivity Lock is the defensibility. Detail in `agentic-roadmap.md`.

## Moat (v5.1 — Two Layers)

### Layer 1 — ConversionOracle™ (Data Moat)

Claude Vision + OCR + geo are features any competent team can rebuild in a weekend. Their value is **not** the pipeline — it is the **ground-truth labels** each verified visit produces.

**Training flywheel:**

| Phase | Cumulative verified events | Model state | Prediction accuracy |
|-------|----------------------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Day 1–60 (Williamsburg Template 0) | **500 → 1,000** | Cold-start: Claude API + rule-based matching priors | Heuristic baseline |
| Month 3–6 | **1,000 → 5,000** | ConversionOracle v1 (fine-tune) — walk-in rate prediction | ±25% |
| Month 6–12 (NYC Dense expansion to 5 neighborhoods) | **10,000 → 25,000** | v2 — walk-in + LTV prediction | ±15% walk-in · ±30% LTV |
| Month 12–24 (Top-5 Metro, 30 neighborhoods) | **50,000 → 200,000** | v3 — cohort-specific, 30s first-campaign ROI prediction | ±10% walk-in |

**Four structural locks:**
1. **Data exclusivity** — walk-in ground truth is not available to any general ads platform.
2. **Cold-start barrier** — a new entrant needs 1,000+ verified events just to train v1 = 6–12 month head-start window per vertical.
3. **Long-tail advantage** — large-lab general models are data-sparse on nano/micro creators; Push's specialist model is denser where it matters.
4. **Compounding cross-neighborhood transfer** — after 10 neighborhoods, cross-neighborhood priors kick in, accelerating Template N launch.

### Layer 2 — Creator Productivity Lock (Behavior Moat)

CreatorGPT embeds inside the creator's workflow: AI brief-writer, virality predictor, auto-generated compliant disclosure via DisclosureBot, and 48-hour payout (industry average 30–60 days). Leaving Push is not a reputation loss — it is a **40–60% drop in $/hour**. This is Uber-style supply-side switching cost, not LinkedIn-style profile stickiness.

**Meta/Google entry rebuttal:** Entering this market requires three things simultaneously — (1) offline walk-in ground truth, (2) a creator-identity network with local micro creators, (3) SMB direct-sales motion. Meta/Google have none. Most likely path is acquisition (Aspire $240M, Captiv8 $180M as precedent). Push's window is to reach 2,000+ merchants / 50,000+ events before a strategic move lands, which pegs an acquisition floor at 5×+ ARR.

## Core Invariants (v5.1)

1. **Only reward verifiable, repeatable value creation.** Can it be verified? Can it be repeated? Does it create real customer value?
2. **Merchant payment unit is the customer (first + repeat).** Never impressions, followers, posts, reach, engagement, or exposure.
3. **Never charge a merchant before completing their free pilot.** "First 10 free" is a hard contract term, 30-day rolling window, no exception.
4. **Every post goes through DisclosureBot before publish.** Bypass = forfeit payout + tier demotion + second offense ban.
5. **AI verification is never bypassed for metric inflation.** Manual review rate is a quality floor, not a crutch.
6. **Creator payout within 48 hours** of verified walk-in.
7. **Founder equity on 4-year vest + 1-year cliff + reverse vesting** — no exceptions, including founders themselves.
8. **No pitch or marketing document contains the word "agency."** Internal documents may use it for contrast; external documents never.
9. **No creator acquisition material uses "10K+ followers" as a floor.** Williamsburg strategy depends on nano/micro density.
10. **Design system (`Design.md`) applies universally.** Strategic pivots don't override UI rules.

## Company-Building Thesis

**End state:** Push is the vertical AI layer that makes local customer acquisition a commoditized, outcome-priced service — the way Stripe made payments a service, Deel made global payroll a service, and Sierra is making ticket resolution a service. Not a platform merchants log into, but an **outcome they buy**. Feature-competitive tools get undercut by ChatGPT; Push compounds on walk-in ground truth (ConversionOracle), a locked operator network (Creator Productivity Lock), and a vertical-specific playbook (Neighborhood Playbook) that competitors cannot rent.

**Benchmark comparisons:**
- Stripe for payments → Push for local customer acquisition
- Sierra for ticket resolution → Push for walk-in acquisition
- Harvey for legal work → Push for storefront demand
- Deel for global hiring → Push for local demand operations
- Uber for supply dispatch → Push for creator-driven walk-ins (outcome, not commodity)

## Beachhead — Williamsburg Coffee+ × 60 Days

**Discipline:** One vertical, one neighborhood, one pitch until Template 0 saturates.

- **Vertical:** Specialty coffee + third-wave cafés with bakery/brunch items (**Coffee+**, AOV $8–$20). Not "F&B." Not "pure coffee" alone — pure-coffee AOV can't carry the unit economics. Coffee+ hits ~200 addressable merchants in Williamsburg (vs ~120 pure coffee).
- **Geography:** Williamsburg, Brooklyn (ZIPs 11211 / 11249). Dense cafe count, heavy Gen-Z foot traffic, high local creator density.
- **Time-box:** 60 days from kickoff to saturation evidence. Saturation = ≥10 paying merchants, ≥20 campaigns, ≥$10K GMV, Retention Add-on attaching on ≥50% of campaigns.
- **Expansion trigger (only after saturation):** Nolita or Brooklyn Heights as Template 1 (NYC Dense tranche); same vertical; tests Neighborhood Playbook transfer assumptions before opening a new vertical.
- **Not yet:** Manhattan mainstream, pure dessert, beauty, retail, fitness. Boutique fitness is counter-seasonal insurance scheduled for M12.

Full 60-day week-by-week plan lives in `push-gtm`. Neighborhood Playbook productization lives in `push-gtm` with economic detail in `push-pricing`.

## Decision Standard

Every major decision must answer YES to at least one:
1. Improves merchant ROI clarity or outcome predictability?
2. Improves creator earning quality or switching cost inside Push (SCOR)?
3. Deepens ConversionOracle training signal per campaign?
4. Reduces verification cost, fraud, or DisclosureBot exposure?
5. Improves Software Leverage Ratio?
6. Reinforces Neighborhood Playbook transferability?

If none → kill it.

## Strategic Red Flags

Push is drifting if it starts to behave like:
- A sample-giveaway app or creator coupon engine
- A posting board / "gigs for creators" directory
- A local discovery map with weak unit economics
- A content engagement platform with shallow verification
- A SaaS dashboard sold on seat count
- An influencer marketplace where creators set their own rates
- An agency pitching "we run your campaigns" without SLR commitments
- A reward / loyalty / points app

If a pitch, landing page, email, or feature sounds like any of the above — stop, reread this file, rewrite. And if the word "agency" appears in any outbound artifact, treat it as a bug and ship the fix same-day.
