# Push Strategy — Overview (v5.0)

> **v5.0 reset (2026-04-18):** Push is no longer positioned as a two-sided marketplace or SaaS platform. Push is an **AI-Powered Customer Acquisition Agency** selling **verified customers** on outcome-based pricing. This file reflects the v5.0 reset — any v4 "marketplace / SaaS / $19.99 tiers / Offer OS / Creator Credit / Demand Surface / Liquidity Layer" language is retired.

---

## Strategic Definition

Push is an **AI operator company** that delivers local customer acquisition as a service. The merchant tells Push how many new customers they need; Push's agent matches AI-managed creators, runs the campaign, verifies every visit with multi-modal AI (Claude Vision + QR OCR + geo), and bills **per verified customer**. Creators are not a marketplace supply — they are Push's AI-scheduled operator network.

**Push IS:** an AI agency for local customer acquisition, a verification layer, an autonomous campaign engine, a compounding performance-data moat.
**Push is NOT:** an influencer marketplace, a creator SaaS tool, a local discovery app, a content community, a loyalty app, a sample-giveaway engine, a directory.

**v5.0 one-line pitch:** "Tell us how many customers you need. AI + our creator network delivers — or it's free."

## Five Strategic Pillars (v5.0)

The 5 v4 pillars (Offer OS / Creator Credit / Attribution / Demand Surface / Liquidity Layer) are **fully retired**. The v5.0 pillars below replace them.

### 1. Outcome Engine
Merchants tell Push **how many customers they need** (e.g. "20 new customers, this week, at my Williamsburg coffee shop"). Push commits to delivery or the campaign is free. Billing unit is the verified customer — never impressions, followers, reach, or posts. This is the commercial core.

### 2. Creator OS
Creators are **Push's AI-scheduled operator network**, not independent marketplace supply. The agent assigns campaigns based on tier, score, geo, category fit, and historical conversion. Tier and score are **non-transferable outside Push** — a creator who leaves Push leaves their reputation behind. This structurally defeats disintermediation.

### 3. AI Verification Layer
**Day-1 multi-modal auto-verification** for every claimed visit: Claude Vision reads the receipt/storefront photo, OCR validates the QR payload, geo-fence confirms location, account/device velocity flags fraud. Roughly 80% auto-approve, 20% human override. Verification is not a Phase-1 feature — it is table stakes from campaign #1.

### 4. Agentic Campaign Engine
Merchant submits a goal → in ~60 seconds the agent returns a creator slate + campaign brief + ROI prediction. Campaign design, matching, brief generation, pricing, timing are all agent-driven from day one. The merchant approves; the agent executes. Human operators handle edge cases and relationships, not dispatch.

### 5. Data Moat
Every campaign trains the matching agent. 500 campaigns teaches category/offer/timing patterns; 5,000+ campaigns builds a moat no competitor starting cold can catch. Proprietary performance data (creator × category × offer × geo × time → verified conversion) is the asset that makes Push irreplaceable, not the software.

## Core Invariants (Never Violate)

1. **Only reward verifiable, repeatable value creation.** Three tests: Can it be verified? Can it be repeated? Does it create real customer value?
2. **Merchant payment unit is the customer.** Never impressions, followers, posts, reach, engagement, or exposure.
3. **Creator credit (tier + score) is non-transferable outside Push.** It is an in-system asset, not a portable résumé.
4. **Never charge a merchant before completing their free pilot.** The $0 Pilot is a promise — violating it destroys the outcome-based narrative.
5. **Design system (`Design.md`) applies universally.** Strategic pivots do not override UI constraints.

## Company-Building Thesis

**End state:** Push is the AI agency layer that makes local customer acquisition a commoditized, outcome-priced service — the way Stripe made payments a service, or Deel made global payroll a service. Not a platform merchants log into, but an **outcome they buy**. Replaceable platforms compete on features; Push compounds on verified performance data and an AI-managed operator network competitors cannot rent.

**Benchmark comparisons:**
- Stripe for payments → Push for local customer acquisition
- Deel for global hiring → Push for local demand operations
- AI agencies (YC Spring 2026 RFS #5 thesis) → Push is the local-commerce instance

## Beachhead — Coffee × Williamsburg × 60 Days

**Discipline:** Only one category, only one neighborhood, only one pitch, until saturation.

- **Category:** Specialty coffee (independent roasters, third-wave cafes, small chains). Not "F&B" — coffee specifically. High visit frequency, photogenic product, strong local-creator overlap, repeat-visit economics.
- **Geography:** Williamsburg (Brooklyn), ZIPs 11211 / 11249. Dense cafe count, heavy Gen-Z foot traffic, high local creator density.
- **Time-box:** 60 days from kickoff to saturation evidence. Saturation = ≥10 merchants, ≥20 campaigns, ≥$10K GMV, repeat rate measurable.
- **Expansion trigger (only after saturation):** Greenpoint (11222) + Bushwick (11206, 11237), same category. No new category until all three ZIPs saturate.
- **Not yet:** Manhattan, dessert, beauty, retail, fitness. Discussing them at this stage is scope creep.

See `push-gtm` skill for the 60-day week-by-week plan.

## Decision Standard

Every major decision must answer YES to at least one:
1. Improves merchant ROI clarity or outcome predictability?
2. Improves creator earning quality or tier mobility inside Push?
3. Deepens the AI data moat (training signal per campaign)?
4. Reduces verification cost, fraud, or friction?
5. Keeps value capture on-platform (anti-disintermediation)?

If a decision doesn't answer YES to at least one, kill it.

## Strategic Red Flags

Push is drifting if it starts to behave like:
- A sample-giveaway app or creator coupon engine
- A posting board / "gigs for creators" directory
- A local discovery map with weak unit economics
- A content engagement platform with shallow verification
- A SaaS dashboard sold on seat count
- An influencer marketplace where creators set their own rates
- A reward / loyalty / points app

If a pitch, landing page, email, or feature starts to sound like any of the above — stop, reread this file, rewrite.
