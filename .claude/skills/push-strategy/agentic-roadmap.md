# Agentic Roadmap — Push 2026–2027 (v5.0)

> **v5.0 reset (2026-04-18):** The old "Phase 0 manual → Phase 1 AI after 50+ campaigns" staged roadmap is retired. Under v5.0, **AI verification is Day-1**, not gated. This file documents the Day-1 stack and the autonomy ladder that follows (matching → full autonomous operation).

---

## Why Agentic (v5.0 Framing)

**The SaaS Winter context:** General AI (Claude, GPT, Gemini) commoditizes software features. Pure "SaaS tool with AI sprinkled on" positioning dies — anyone can clone a feature list in a weekend.

**Push survives because it sells outcomes.** The unit of sale is a **verified customer**, not a seat. AI is not a marketing adjective — it is the execution layer. Every campaign's verification, matching, and brief generation run through models from campaign #1. Competitors cloning a feature list still need 500+ campaigns of proprietary performance data before their matching works. They cannot rent that.

**Position:** Push is an **AI-Powered Customer Acquisition Agency** (YC Spring 2026 RFS #5). AI is the product. The creator network is the AI-managed operator layer. The merchant buys outcomes.

## Day-1 AI Architecture (Live from Campaign #1)

There is no "manual phase." On campaign #1, merchants experience a system where every claimed visit goes through multi-modal verification and every creator slate is generated by the matching agent. Humans handle exceptions (~20%), not the hot path.

### Day-1 Verification Stack (runs on every claimed visit)

| Layer | Model / Method | What It Checks | Signal |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Vision** | Claude Vision (Sonnet 4.5 multimodal) | Storefront match, receipt legibility, in-store photo authenticity | Visual confidence score 0–1 |
| **OCR** | Vision + text extraction | QR-encoded campaign ID matches claim; receipt item/amount match offer | Hard match / soft match / fail |
| **Geo** | Geofence + device coords | Scan location within merchant radius; movement plausibility | Within-fence bool + velocity check |
| **Fraud** | Account + device velocity, graph signals | Repeat-account clusters, bot-pattern cadence, impossible timelines | Risk score 0–1 |
| **Aggregation** | Weighted combination | Combines the above into auto-approve / auto-reject / human-review buckets | Decision + confidence |

**Thresholds (initial, tune per campaign-1000 data):**
- Auto-approve: combined confidence ≥ 0.85
- Human review: 0.50 – 0.85 (creator relations + merchant ops triage)
- Auto-reject: < 0.50 (with appeal path)

**Target mix from Day-1:** ~80% auto-decide, ~20% human override. The 20% is not Phase-0 "we haven't built AI yet" — it is the deliberate human-in-the-loop quality floor.

### Day-1 Agentic Campaign Engine

Merchant submits a goal ("20 new customers, Williamsburg, coffee, within 7 days") and in ~60 seconds the agent returns:
- A creator slate (5–15 matched operators, ranked by tier + score + geo + category fit)
- A draft campaign brief (offer structure, content guidelines, timing window, payout per verified customer)
- An ROI prediction (expected verified customers, confidence band, spend estimate)

Merchant approves / edits → agent dispatches → verification stack runs on each claim → payout on auto-approve.

From day one. Not after 50 campaigns.

### Why "Day-1 AI" Is Defensible (Not Theater)

Day-1 AI only works because:
1. Verification models (Claude Vision, OCR, geo) are **general-purpose** — they work with zero Push-specific training data on day one.
2. The **matching agent** starts with heuristic priors (tier + geo + category fit) and improves with every campaign. It is never "dumb" — it just gets smarter.
3. Fraud detection is seeded with **published adversarial patterns** (bot-farm signatures, geo-impossibility heuristics), then refines.
4. Human review is a **quality floor**, not a crutch — it exists even at scale for edge cases and appeals.

What we do **not** claim on day one: "Our matching agent is smarter than a human buyer." We claim: "Our matching agent gives you a slate in 60 seconds, end-to-end verified, billed per customer." Those two claims carry the pitch without overreach.

## Autonomy Ladder (Evolves With Data)

Autonomy increases with campaign volume. What does **not** change: verification is Day-1 AI, billing is per-customer, creator credit is non-portable.

### Level 1 — Foundation (Campaigns 1–50)

- **Verification:** Day-1 multi-modal stack live; ~80% auto, 20% human.
- **Matching:** Agent uses heuristic priors (tier + geo + category). Humans spot-check every slate before dispatch.
- **Campaign design:** Agent drafts brief; human (Jiaming / Prum) edits before merchant sees it.
- **Operations cost per campaign:** ~3–5 human hours (outreach, slate review, edge-case handling).

**Exit signal:** 50 campaigns completed, repeat-merchant evidence (≥2 merchants run 3+ campaigns), fraud patterns mapped, auto-verification confidence stable ≥80%.

### Level 2 — Trained Matching (Campaigns 50–500)

- **Verification:** Same Day-1 stack, with thresholds tuned on real data.
- **Matching:** Agent now trained on **verified performance data** (creator × category × offer × geo × time → conversion). Human review drops to spot-checks on slates flagged by confidence < 0.70.
- **Campaign design:** Agent generates brief from templates derived from top-performing campaigns. Human edits only on custom requests.
- **Operations cost per campaign:** ~1–2 human hours.

**Exit signal:** Matching prediction vs. actual conversion within ±15%, merchant-approved-slate rate ≥80%, ops cost stable.

### Level 3 — Autonomous Operation (Campaigns 500+)

- **Verification:** Same Day-1 stack; thresholds may tighten as fraud patterns are learned.
- **Matching:** Agent auto-dispatches on standard requests; human review only for novel categories, novel geographies, or high-value (>$10K GMV) campaigns.
- **Campaign design → execution → payout → reporting:** End-to-end agent-run for typical campaigns.
- **Dashboard:** Merchants see real-time campaign state; comms move from email to in-app.
- **Operations cost per campaign:** <30 human minutes on typical, 2–4 hours on edge cases.

**Exit signal:** ≥90% of campaigns run end-to-end without human intervention; satisfaction/repeat rate holds or rises.

### Escalation / Fallback (Always-On)

Every level has the same escalation rules — humans always handle:
- Novel merchant category or geography (no prior data)
- High-value disputes (> $10K impact)
- Systemic fraud (new attack vector, not yet modeled)
- Creator grievances / appeals (human is the court of record)

## 5-Person Team Leverage Under Day-1 AI

Under v5.0 the team does **not** do manual verification or manual matching on day one — AI handles the hot path. Humans handle **relationship, strategy, and exception work** from the start.

### Current Roles (Day-1)

| Person | v5.0 Focus | What AI Handles For Them |
|--------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Jiaming** (Strategy / Ops) | Merchant strategy, positioning, pilot closes, edge cases | Campaign brief drafts, matching slate first pass, ROI predictions |
| **Z** (Engineering) | Verification stack, agent infra, data pipeline, model tuning | — (Z builds the AI; AI saves everyone else's hours) |
| **Lucy** (Creator Relations) | Creator recruiting (Williamsburg coffee), tier progression, appeals, high-value disputes | QR verification, fraud triage on standard cases, creator-facing notifications |
| **Prum** (Merchant Ops) | Merchant onboarding, pilot-to-paid conversion, repeat-campaign expansion | Dispute triage, merchant status updates, repeat-campaign brief generation |
| **Milly** (Analytics / Brand) | Performance storytelling, competitive positioning, brand | Reporting generation, pattern mining, dashboard data |

### Time Saved vs. a World Where Verification Is Manual

A manual verification world burns ~10–15 hours/week/person on receipt checks, dispute triage, and slate-assembly drudgery. Under Day-1 AI, that time is redirected to relationship and strategy from campaign #1 — which is why a 5-person team can run a Williamsburg coffee pilot credibly.

**Key Principle:** AI doesn't replace people. It replaces **drudgery**. The team's job is outcomes and relationships, not receipt-reading.

## Risk Mitigation

### Verification Risk: Gamed AI / Synthetic Proofs
- **Mitigation:** Human spot-check 10% of auto-approves continuously; fraud playbook updated monthly; combined multi-signal verification (vision + OCR + geo + velocity — gaming all four is hard and leaves tells).

### Matching Risk: Bad Slate → Merchant Blames Push
- **Mitigation:** Merchant always sees the slate **before** dispatch and can edit; confidence bands shown on every match; early-stage campaigns get a human second-look on every slate.

### Autonomy Risk: Scaling Before Quality Holds
- **Mitigation:** Each level-up (1→2, 2→3) has hard exit signals; if satisfaction or verification accuracy drops, we revert a level and retrain, we do not push through.

### Narrative Risk: "AI-Washing" Accusations
- **Mitigation:** Day-1 AI is **demonstrable** — a reporter or investor can watch a live verification + matching run in <5 minutes. We never claim autonomy beyond what we can show.

## Data Moat (Compounding From Campaign #1)

Every campaign contributes a verified performance record:
`(creator_id, tier, score) × (merchant_category, offer_type, time_window, geo) → (verified_customers, conversion_rate, dispute_rate)`

After 100 campaigns, matching priors are measurably better than heuristics.
After 500 campaigns, category/offer/timing patterns are defensible.
After 5,000 campaigns (target Year 2), a competitor starting from zero cannot catch up without running the same volume. This is the moat. It is not a feature; it is an accumulated asset.

Combined with creator-credit non-portability (Creator OS pillar), the moat is dual-locked: data compounds on Push's side, and creators cannot take their scores elsewhere.
