## §2. Scope of Agreement

This Agreement applies from the Effective Date for the duration of Founder's service to Push and indefinitely thereafter as to confidentiality obligations and already-assigned inventions. Subsequent employment agreements or equity grants do not supersede Sections 3–5 unless explicitly stated.

---

## §3. Assignment of Inventions

**3.1 Work Inventions.** Founder hereby assigns to Push all right, title, and interest worldwide in and to any invention, discovery, improvement, trade secret, work of authorship, method, algorithm, model, data, design, or other intellectual property (collectively, "Inventions") that Founder conceives, reduces to practice, or develops, solely or jointly, during Founder's service to Push **if** such Invention (a) relates to Push's actual or reasonably anticipated business, (b) results from work performed for Push, or (c) is created with Push's resources or confidential information.

**3.2 Moral Rights Waiver.** To the maximum extent permitted by law, Founder waives any moral rights in Inventions assigned to Push.

**3.3 Disclosure Obligation.** Founder will promptly disclose all Inventions to Push in writing.

**3.4 Further Assurances.** Founder will execute any further documents, including patent/copyright assignments and declarations, that Push reasonably requests to perfect Push's ownership. Founder appoints Push as attorney-in-fact for this limited purpose if Founder is unavailable.

---

## §4. Prior Inventions

**4.1 Exclusions.** The following pre-existing inventions of Founder (the "Prior Inventions") are NOT assigned to Push under this Agreement. Founder represents that all Prior Inventions are listed on **Exhibit A** below. If Exhibit A is blank, Founder represents that there are no Prior Inventions.

**4.2 Limited License.** If Founder incorporates any Prior Invention into a Push product or service without assigning it, Founder grants Push a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free, worldwide, non-exclusive license to use, modify, and sublicense such Prior Invention as incorporated.

---

## §5. Confidentiality

**5.1 Definition.** "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of Push to Founder, whether orally, in writing, or by observation, including technical information (code, algorithms, models, training data, ConversionOracle specifications), business information (merchant/creator lists, pricing, financial projections), and strategic information (roadmap, competitive analyses), together with any derivatives.

**5.2 Obligation.** Founder will hold Confidential Information in strict confidence, use it solely for Push's benefit, and not disclose it to any third party except as authorized by Push in writing. Confidentiality obligations survive termination of Founder's service **indefinitely for trade secrets** and **for a period of [five (5) years]** for non-trade-secret Confidential Information.

**5.3 Exclusions.** Confidential Information excludes information that: (a) is in the public domain through no fault of Founder; (b) was lawfully in Founder's possession before receipt from Push; (c) is received from a third party without restriction; or (d) is independently developed by Founder without use of Confidential Information.

**5.4 Whistleblower Carve-Out.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, pursuant to 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b) (Defend Trade Secrets Act), Founder will not be held criminally or civilly liable under federal or state trade-secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that (a) is made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official or to an attorney solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law, or (b) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding if made under seal.

---

## §6. Return of Materials

Upon termination of service, Founder will return to Push all Push property, including all copies of Confidential Information and any materials containing Inventions. Founder will not retain any copies, notes, or extracts.

---

## §7. No Conflict

Founder represents that:
- Founder's service to Push does not and will not breach any agreement with a prior employer, contractor, or third party.
- No pre-existing invention-assignment, non-disclosure, or restrictive-covenant obligation prevents Founder from fulfilling this Agreement.
- Founder will not bring any third-party confidential information onto Push premises or use it in Push work.

---

## §8. Non-Solicitation of Employees and Contractors

For a period of **twelve (12) months** following termination of Founder's service, Founder will not directly or indirectly solicit any then-current employee or active contractor of Push to leave Push or to work for Founder or any third party, except through general public advertisements not specifically targeted at Push personnel.

---

## §9. Non-Compete — [INTENTIONALLY OMITTED]

Consistent with the FTC's 2024 non-compete rule and New York State Human Rights Law (and the general inadvisability of non-competes for professional services in California or under the Federal Trade Commission's policy statements), this Agreement does **not** contain a post-service non-compete clause. Founder's ongoing obligations are carried by Sections 3 (assignment), 5 (confidentiality), and 8 (non-solicitation). Counsel review: confirm this approach aligns with current state and federal guidance at time of signature.

---

## §10. Governing Law; Dispute Resolution

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware without regard to conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising under this Agreement will be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in New York County, New York. Each party waives any objection based on personal jurisdiction, venue, or forum non conveniens.

---

## §11. Miscellaneous

**11.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with any founder's stock purchase agreement, equity plan, and the Company's Bylaws, constitutes the entire understanding between the parties regarding its subject matter.

**11.2 Amendment.** Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties. No oral waiver is effective.

**11.3 Severability.** If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect, and the unenforceable provision will be reformed to the minimum extent necessary.

**11.4 Assignment by Company.** Push may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all its assets. Founder may not assign.

**11.5 Survival.** Sections 3, 5, 6, 8, 10, and 11 survive termination.
